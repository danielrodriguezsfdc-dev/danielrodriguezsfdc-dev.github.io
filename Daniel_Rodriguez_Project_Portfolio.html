Contact Lifecycle Tracking System

Skills: Solution DesignData ArchitectureAutomationIntegration
Tools: FlowZoomInfoLinkedIn Sales NavigatorEinstein Activity Capture

Situation

Company sold largely through AE networks and existing relationships. AEs were manually tracking contact job changes but couldn't cover everyone or react fast enough. Sales requested a systematic way to monitor these contacts. Separately, contact data was degrading — company and title information only updated when AEs manually edited records in Salesforce.

Task

Build a system to detect contact job changes, match to existing accounts, update records automatically, and alert the right people. Solve the broader data hygiene problem at the same time, keeping contact company and title data current.

Action

  • Designed multi-component system: (1) Data ingestion from ZoomInfo and LinkedIn to detect job title/company changes; (2) Auto-matching logic to link contacts to existing accounts when clear match exists; (3) Manual resolution UI for ambiguous cases; (4) Alert system filtered by contact relevance score (built separately - see Contact Priority Scoring); (5) Record linking to preserve EAC activity history while keeping email consistent

Result

AEs automatically alerted when contacts changed jobs. Improved contact data hygiene across the org, company and title fields stayed current without manual updates. Marketing could segment contacts at new companies for targeted campaigns. Preserved relationship context across job changes.

MEDDIC Data Architecture Redesign

Skills: Data ArchitectureData Quality & GovernanceStakeholder ManagementProcess DesignAutomation
Tools: FlowValidation RulesDynamic Forms

Situation

MEDDIC field completion on Accounts with open Opportunities was low. Sales leadership requested making fields required.

Task

Improve MEDDIC data completion without breaking the sales process.

Action

  • Pushed back on original request — fields weren't relevant for every opportunity or stage; blanket requirement would create friction and bad data
  • Proposed revised architecture: Opportunities hold point-in-time MEDDIC (snapshot at deal), Accounts hold latest MEDDIC (rolled up from most recent open Opportunity)
  • Built automation for Opportunity → Account rollup with conditional logic and recency checks
  • Implemented conditional validation: different fields required at different stages, varying by record type
  • Extended pattern to Tech Partner fields

Result

Achieved 100% MEDDIC completion on closed opportunities through stage-based validation, without adding friction to early-stage deals. Cleaner data model preserving historical context while surfacing current state. Pattern reused for Tech Partner fields.

Sales Process Analysis

Skills: Data Quality & GovernanceReporting & AnalysisStakeholder Management
Tools: Flow

Situation

RevOps wanted to analyze the relationship between data hygiene and opportunity win rates and velocity.

Task

Retrieve data, analyze, and surface actionable insights.

Action

  • Delivered the initial analysis, then noticed anomalies worth investigating — RevOps VP gave me room to dig deeper
  • Found AEs under-creating opportunities (only logging confident deals), except for demos which required an opp — skewing win rates
  • Found renewal opportunities frequently created as new business, further skewing win rates
  • Proposed validation rules at opportunity creation, monitoring reports on manager dashboards, and automation to set correct opportunity type for renewals

Result

Initial request fulfilled. Secondary findings led to a sales process revamp — standardized opp creation requirements, automated renewal classification, and accurate win rate data for the first time.
